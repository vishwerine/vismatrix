The Eisenhower Matrix

Not all tasks are created equal. The Eisenhower Matrix helps you prioritize by categorizing tasks into four quadrants:

Urgent & Important

Do these immediately. Crises, deadlines, emergencies.

Action: Do it now

Important, Not Urgent

Schedule these. Planning, relationships, learning.

Action: Schedule it

Urgent, Not Important

Delegate if possible. Interruptions, some emails.

Action: Delegate it

Neither Urgent nor Important

Eliminate these. Time wasters, busy work.

Action: Delete it

Time Blocking Technique

Instead of a to-do list, schedule every hour of your day. Assign specific time blocks to specific tasks. This creates structure and prevents time from slipping away.

6:00 AM - 7:00 AM Morning routine & exercise
9:00 AM - 11:00 AM Deep work (most important task)
11:00 AM - 12:00 PM Emails & admin tasks
2:00 PM - 4:00 PM Meetings & collaboration

Eliminate Time Wasters

The average person wastes 2-3 hours daily on non-essential activities. Here's how to reclaim that time:

  • Turn off all non-essential notifications
  • Batch similar tasks together
  • Learn to say "no" to non-priorities
  • Limit social media to specific times
  • Reduce unnecessary meetings

80/20 Rule

20% of your activities produce 80% of your results. Identify and focus on your highest-leverage activities.

Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute intervals followed by 5-minute breaks. After 4 intervals, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This maintains high focus and prevents burnout.
